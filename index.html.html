The Studio

A practice built on memory, material and place.

Hiraya — the fruit of one's hopes, dreams and aspirations.

Hiraya Design Studio is the architecture and interior design practice of Apeksha Naik. Trained in architecture at Manipal University and shaped by early years working alongside established practices in Bangalore, Apeksha returned to the coast to build homes the way this region has always built them — with care, character and continuity.

The studio works across new residences, restorations and interiors, with a particular love for ancestral homes: the ones worth saving, and the new ones worth building in their memory.

Materials & Craft

Every material in a Hiraya home is chosen to age well — and most of them have already proven they can.

Athangudi tile

Handmade in Tamil Nadu the way it has been for generations — laid here in patterns drawn for each room, not picked from a catalogue.

Chettinad stone & timber

Columns and doorways sourced and restored, carrying a century of craft into homes built for the next one.

Solid wood rafter ceilings

Retained wherever they exist, rebuilt where they don't — the warmth overhead that flat concrete never learned.

Red oxide & lime

Floors and walls finished by hand, cool underfoot, alive to light — materials that belong to this coast.

Cane, rattan & antique restoration

Old furniture repaired and repurposed rather than replaced; new pieces woven by local hands.

The Process

Slow by intention.

i.

Listen

Every home starts with a long conversation — about the family, the land, and what the house needs to remember.

ii.

Design

Drawings develop unhurried, tested against light, climate and the way the family actually lives.

iii.

Detail

Materials are sourced, craftspeople chosen, and every junction resolved before work begins on site.

iv.

Build

The studio stays on site through construction — because the last five percent is where a house becomes a home.
